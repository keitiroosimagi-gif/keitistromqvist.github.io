Keiti Strömqvist

Mezzo-Soprano

Biography

Keiti Strömqvist is a Swedish-Estonian mezzo-soprano, active as both an operatic and concert soloist. She has appeared in repertoire ranging from Baroque to contemporary music, with a particular affinity for Mozart, Rossini, and dramatic mezzo roles.

Her operatic portrayals include Carmen (Carmen, Bizet / Luup), Angelina (La Cenerentola), Cherubino (Le nozze di Figaro) and Auntie (Peter Grimes). On the concert stage, she has performed works such as Mozart’s Requiem, Haydn’s Nelson Mass, Handel’s Messiah, and Bach’s St John Passion.

Keiti has appeared throughout Sweden, Denmark, and Estonia, including performances at Göteborgs Stadsteater and the PULSAR Festival. She is a member of the Göteborg Symphony Orchestra Vocal Ensemble and has worked as an extra chorister with Göteborg Opera.

She holds a Bachelor’s degree from the Royal Danish Academy of Music in Copenhagen and will begin her Master’s studies in Opera at the Academy of Music and Drama in Gothenburg.
